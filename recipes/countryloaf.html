Countryloaf Recipe


Description

A delicious take on the classic take on the french countryloaf sourdough recipe. A simple mix of flour, water, sourdough and voil√†!

Ingredients

  • 800g of Bread Flour
  • 460g of water ( just below room temp.)
  • ~12g of salt
  • 325g of Sourdough Starter

Steps

  1. Mix your dry ingredients together with a whisk, until nicely blended.
  2. Mix the blended dry ingredients with your Sourdough, until a crumbly consistency.
  3. Let the mixture sit fo ~10-15 mins, as to form some gluten structure to mix better later on
  4. Add the water and kneed until it passes the window-pane test (depending on your flour, add less of the water)
  5. Let the final dough sit for 4-5 hours until doubled in size
  6. Cover your workspace with a thin layer of flour to place the dough
  7. Cut the dough into 2 pieces (or 3 for smaller loafs)
  8. Shape the pieces into loafs
  9. Place shaped pieces into nicely floured bread-bannetons or appropriately sized tupperware
  10. Cover the loafs in a bad and place in the fridge for ~8-12 hours
  11. Before next steps, preheat oven to 480-490C
  12. Take loafs out of fridge place into a dutch oven, cut bread on the top side or center with a leif (razor will do just fine)
  13. Place bread into for 40mins, make sure to place a tin of water to humidify the oven for abetter oven-spring
  14. Remove the lid of the dutch-oven for 2-3 mins for browning and blistering
  15. Take out loafs and let them rest for an hour
  16. Enjoy :)
