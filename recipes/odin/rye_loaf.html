Rye Loaf Recipe


Description

You've heard about the fabled Rye flour, have you? Working with it...'tis dark magic, I tell you! But if you're up for the task, I'll fill you in on my secrets. May its dark, dense and delightful flavours fill your belly!

Ingredients

  • 500g of Rye Flour
  • ~360g of Water (depending on the quality of flour, less water may be needed)
  • ~10-13g of salt
  • 360g of Sourdough Starter (preferably sourdough fed rye/wholewheat flour)
  • Patience and perseverance.

Steps

  1. Mix your dry ingredients together with a whisk, until nicely blended.
  2. Mix the blended dry ingredients with your Sourdough, until a crumbly consistency.
  3. Let the mixture sit fo ~20-30 mins to proof, as to form some gluten structure to mix better later on
  4. Add the water and kneed until you get very heavy and thick paste (depending on your flour, add less of the water), Rye dough does not feel like regular dough due to its low gluten content
  5. Shape into a ball, place it in a bowl , lightly oil it with olive oil, cover it with cling wrap, and let it rest for 2.5 hours at room temperature.
  6. Take out dough into a floured surface, and knock back the air, expect it to crack as you reshape it into ball.
  7. Place the shaped dough into a floured bread-banneton or tupperware. Let it sit for ~2 hours
  8. Before next steps, preheat oven to 480-490C
  9. Take loaf out of fridge place into a dutch oven, no need to cut into dough, the rye dough will naturally form cracks
  10. Place bread into for 40-50mins, make sure to place a tin of water to humidify the oven for abetter oven-spring
  11. Remove the lid of the dutch-oven for 2-3 mins for browning and blistering
  12. Take out loaf and let it rest ~10 hours to a day before cutting. This is an important step, as Rye tends to be bit gummier.
  13. If you've patiently waited, you are now able to enjoy the fruit of thy labor.
