Sourdough Starter Recipe


Description

You wanna start a Sourdough Starter of your own, eh? Well look no further. I've got you.

Ingredients

  • ~1500g of Wholewheat/Multigrain Flour (i advice against using all-purpose flour, though it should work)
  • Water

Steps

  1. Make sure to follow these steps around the same time of day
  2. First Day: In a small bowl, place 50g of flour and 50g of water. Mix until combined, cover it and leave it overnight at room temperature.
  3. Second Day: Add 50g of flour and 50 of water, mix it, cover it and leave it overnight at room temperature.
  4. Third Day: Throw out 100g of Starter. Mix in 100g of equal parts water and flour into leftover. Mix it, cover it and leave it overnight at room temperature.
  5. Fourth Day: Throw out 150g of Starter. Mix in 150g of equal parts water and flour into leftover. Mix it, cover it and leave it overnight at room temperature. Starter should start to smell. May contain small bubbles
  6. Fifth Day: Throw out 200g of Starter. Mix in 200g of equal parts water and flour into leftover. Mix it, cover it and leave it overnight at room temperature. Starter should be more active and contain bubbles.
  7. Sixth Day: Throw out 250g of Starter. Mix in 250g of equal parts water and flour into leftover. Mix it, cover it and leave it overnight at room temperature.
  8. Seventh Day: Congrats! You did it, you actually stuck to it. You should have a working Sourdough Starter ready for use! Like NOW!
  9. Either feed the starter everyday, half its bodyweight in equal parts water and flour. Or feed it once a week by keeping if in the fridge. Or once a month by freezing it. Or every 6 months by drying it out completely.
